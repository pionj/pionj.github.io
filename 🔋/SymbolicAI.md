In AI, interest in symbolic and statistical approaches comes in waves. You studied during one of the symbolic waves, and currently, we are in a statistical wave. There is no doubt that it will be followed by tremendous interest in symbolic approaches again.

The challenge is to have everything ready when interest in Prolog resurges. Statistical artificial intelligence (AI) and symbolic AI are two different approaches to building intelligent systems. Statistical AI, also known as machine learning, focuses on creating systems that can learn from and make predictions or decisions based on data. This approach uses statistical techniques to identify patterns and relationships in data, and then uses these patterns to make predictions or decisions. Examples of statistical AI techniques include neural networks, support vector machines, and clustering algorithms. Symbolic AI, on the other hand, focuses on encoding knowledge and reasoning rules into the system. This approach represents knowledge using symbols and uses rules to manipulate these symbols to perform tasks such as reasoning, planning, and problem-solving. Symbolic AI is often used in expert systems and knowledge-based systems. In summary, statistical AI relies on learning from data to make predictions or decisions, while symbolic AI focuses on encoding knowledge and reasoning rules to perform tasks. Both approaches have their strengths and weaknesses, and researchers often combine them to create more powerful AI systems.
